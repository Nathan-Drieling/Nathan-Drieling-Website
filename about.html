About Me

My college academics began with two years at Mississippi Gulf Coast Community College. From the fall of 2012 to the fall of 2014, I went to both the Harrison County and the Jackson County Campuses. While attending, I persued (but did not attain) a Bachelor of Science degree in Pre-Physical Therapy.

In the spring of 2015, I switched majors and transfered to the University of Southern Mississippi Gulf Park Campus. Here, I learned various programming langauges and technologies. My favorite classes of study were Artificial Intelligence, Database Management Systems, and Algorithms. I graduated with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science in the spring of 2019.

In the summer of 2018, I had the opprotunity to intern with the National Data Buoy Center located within NASA John C. Stennis Space Center. Here I gained real world software development experience by creating a database and testing it for a new front end software system for the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's operations. On the last week, I gave a presentation in front of the NDBC staff where I demonstrated the database along with all the test code, graphs, or charts which I produced.
